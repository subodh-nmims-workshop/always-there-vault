## The Problem Nobody Talks About

Imagine this: You've spent years building your digital life. Cryptocurrency wallets worth thousands, family photos stored in the cloud, important documents, business secrets, and access to accounts that keep your life running. Then one day, you're gone. What happens to all of it?

This isn't a hypothetical problem. It's happening right now, every day.

**Real stories**:

### The Traditional Solution Doesn't Work

You might think: "Just write down your passwords and put them in a safe." But that creates new problems:

1. **Security Risk**: Physical documents can be stolen, lost in fires, or found by the wrong people
2. **Outdated Information**: Passwords change, accounts get added, information becomes stale
3. **Trust Issues**: Who do you trust with everything? Your lawyer? Family member? What if they betray you?
4. **Legal Complexity**: Traditional wills take months to execute, require lawyers, and cost thousands
5. **Geographic Limitations**: What if your beneficiaries are in different countries?

### The Centralized Service Problem

Some companies offer "digital vault" services, but they have critical flaws:

- **Single Point of Failure**: If the company goes bankrupt, your data is gone
- **Privacy Concerns**: They can see all your sensitive information
- **Hacking Risk**: Centralized databases are honeypots for hackers
- **Censorship**: They can freeze or delete your account
- **Subscription Trap**: Stop paying, lose everything

## The Technical Innovation

### Shamir Secret Sharing

We use a technique invented by cryptographer Adi Shamir:
- Your encryption key is split into 5 pieces
- Any 3 pieces can reconstruct the key
- But 2 or fewer pieces reveal nothing

**This means**:
- No single entity has your key
- Multiple parties must cooperate
- Even if 2 pieces are compromised, you're safe

### Smart Contract Automation

Blockchain smart contracts:
- Execute automatically when conditions are met
- Can't be stopped or censored
- Are transparent and verifiable
- Work 24/7 without human intervention

### Client-Side Encryption

Everything is encrypted in your browser:
- Server never sees your data
- Even if hacked, attackers get nothing
- You maintain complete privacy
